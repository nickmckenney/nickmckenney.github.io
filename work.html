I was previously working at a Google Data Center as a hardware technician and now I have a passion for networks and continuing to work on my home server projects.

Future Technologies Inc

I came onto the Future Technologies team with zero experience in C# and .NET framework. After a month of learning, I am contributing daily to our client's projects. Working in an agile environment has helped workflow and keep the team organized. The .NET framework is great for getting a project started quickly with its built-in features that make development a lot easier.

Developing in a different stack was initially a challenge since the framework used was not the traditional MVC framework used by most web developers.

The Motley Fool

This role was my first software developer job after my graduation from my coding bootcamp. Coming from a Javascript background I had to learn PHP before the start of the internship. Lando and Docker was used daily during the internship since it was used for local development. Apart from contributing to the Global Teams websites daily the most important lesson I learned from the 2 short months was understanding clean code and how it affects the entire teams workflow. Writing clean code is something that for most of the time doesn't need to be explained by comments. Respecting how long methods should be as well as classes can help the entire team. Everyday at the internship started with a daily stand-up explaining what issues we have come across and plans for the future development.

The longest project I worked on with The Motley Fool Global team was an email verification. In the past global users could enter any unverified email through our newsletter to get access to preminum content. This was an issue since our email capture was low and could eventually hurt email marketing. This feature I added to the site required a refresher on AJAX since I needed to communicate both my client-side Javascript and PHP server-side to the browser. The page refreshing to the preminum content was an issue that was solved with AJAX. Other projects I worked on with The Motley Fool were creating a Django quiz app to help bring in level 1 members. Also working with other teams to create scripts to organize JSON objects was a great learning experiance since I never pharsed objects into a CSV file.
